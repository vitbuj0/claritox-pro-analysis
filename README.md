## Side Effects and Safety: What to Know Before You Swallow Anything

Most people tolerate Claritox Pro fine. But "most" isn't "all," and there are some real things to watch.

**GI issues** are the most common complaint. Berberine and concentrated green tea extract can cause nausea, stomach cramps, or loose stools — especially on an empty stomach. Taking the capsules with food usually fixes this.

**Blood pressure and blood sugar interactions** deserve attention. Berberine, chromium, and banaba all affect glucose metabolism. Korean ginseng can nudge blood pressure around. If you're on antihypertensives, diabetes meds, or blood thinners (resveratrol and ginseng both have antiplatelet potential), talk to your doctor. This isn't legal-disclaimer boilerplate — these interactions are documented.

**The green tea liver thing.** Rare, but real. High-concentration EGCG supplements have been linked to hepatotoxicity in a small number of cases. If you have pre-existing liver issues or you're taking anything else that taxes your liver, this is worth a conversation with a healthcare provider.

**Sleep disruption.** A minority of users report restlessness or insomnia, likely from ginseng or any residual caffeine in the green tea extract. Take it earlier in the day if you're sensitive.

Pregnant or nursing? Don't. There's virtually no safety data for multi-botanical combos in pregnancy.
